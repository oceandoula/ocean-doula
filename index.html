My Story

A journey from experience to expertise.

01

First Birth — Finding Power in the Unknown

My first baby was born in 2023. I had planned a home birth — but found myself in a highly medicalised hospital system instead.

In the middle of that pressure and uncertainty, I learned to speak up for myself, to understand medical decisions, and to fight for my choices — one moment at a time. I ended up birthing unmedicated in the hospital bathroom. It was one of the most powerful experiences of my life.

That experience set me on the path to becoming a doula.

02

Second Birth — A Gentle Caesarean, with Full Agency

In 2025, I welcomed my second baby. This time, I was diagnosed with complete placenta praevia — and found myself back at the centre of the medical system.

But this time, I came with the knowledge and tools of a trained doula. I navigated the space between medical safety and personal expectations — and had a caesarean birth that was safe, respected, and powerful.

Both experiences live in everything I teach and every woman I support.

What I Believe

Every baby deserves to arrive in the warmth of their mother's calm and love. Every mother can combine scientific understanding and inner instinct to make choices that are truly her own.

Birth is not just an outcome. It's a process that can be supported, understood, and reclaimed — one in which you are always at the centre.

Frequently Asked

What does a doula actually do?

A doula provides continuous non-medical support before, during, and after birth. I offer physical comfort techniques, emotional encouragement, and help you navigate your options — so you feel informed and empowered throughout your journey.

Do you support medicated and unmedicated births?

Absolutely. My role is to support YOUR birth, whatever form that takes — epidural, unmedicated, planned caesarean, VBAC, or anything in between. No judgment, only support.

Where are you based and who can you support?

I'm based in Perth, Australia and offer 100% virtual doula support — which means I can be with you wherever you are in the world. Whether you're a Mandarin-speaking family in Taiwan or an expat anywhere globally, I'm here for you.

When should I hire a doula?

Many families book in the second trimester (14–27 weeks), but it's never too early or too late. I take a limited number of clients each month, so reaching out sooner helps secure your due-date window.

Do you offer sliding-scale pricing?

Yes. I believe quality doula care should be accessible. Please reach out privately and we can discuss a fee that works for your family.
